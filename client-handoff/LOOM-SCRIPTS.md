# Loom scripts — Live Well (Sandy)

Short scripts for Carson to record. **One Loom per task, ~45–75 seconds each.** Keep them
separate and short — research is clear that one video *per editable element* beats a single
long tour (people re-watch the 60-sec clip for the exact thing they're doing).

Recording tips:
- Screen-share the **real** Pages CMS editor on Sandy's site, not a slide.
- Talk like you're sitting next to her. Slow. Name the button before you click it.
- End every clip with: **"Click the green Save button, then your site updates in about a minute."**
- Host them unlisted (Loom/YouTube) and paste the link **next to the matching field** in the
  editor (Pages CMS field `description`) and in `CLIENT-GUIDE.md`.

---

## 0. "How to get into your editor" (the login one — record this first)
> This is the single biggest source of confused calls for this audience, so go slow.

"Hi Sandy! To change anything on your website, you go to one place. Open your web browser and
go to **app.pagescms.org**. Click **Sign in**, and use the email invite I sent you — just click
the link in that email the first time. After that, it remembers you. Once you're in, you'll see
your website's name. Click it, and you're in your editor. That's the only address you ever need —
I'd save it as a bookmark. That's it for getting in; the next videos show the actual changes."

---

## 1. "Change your phone number or email" (~45 sec)
"Let's update your contact info. On the left side, click **Contact Details (footer)**. You'll
see a few boxes — **City and state**, **Email address**, **Phone number**. To change your phone,
click in the **Phone number** box, delete what's there, and type the new one. If you'd rather not
show a phone at all, just leave it empty. When it looks right, click the **green Save button** at
the top. Your site updates in about a minute. Done."

---

## 2. "Swap the photo at the top of your site" (~60 sec)
"Let's put your photo at the top of your page. On the left, click **Home Page (top of site)**.
The very first box is **Your photo (top of the page)**. Click it, then click **Upload**, and pick
the photo from your computer or phone. Give it a second to load — you'll see a little preview.
That's it. Hit the **green Save button**, wait about a minute, and refresh your site — your photo
is now in the frame at the top. If you ever want to remove it, click the little ✕ on the photo and
the pretty leaf drawing comes back. You can't break anything here."

---

## 3. "Change a monthly pick" (~60 sec)
"This is the part you'll use most — the picks in your **This season** section. On the left, click
**This Season's Picks**. You'll see your list. Click the one you want to change — say **Steady &
Bright**. Now you can edit the **Name of the pick**, the little label above it, and the
**Description** (that types just like a Word document). When it's right, click **Save**, wait a
minute, refresh — updated."

---

## 4. "Add a new monthly pick" (~60 sec)
"To add a brand-new pick: click **This Season's Picks** on the left, then the **+ Add** button at
the top. Fill in the **Name of the pick**, a little label, and a **Description**. The **Display
order** box decides where it shows — 1 is first, 2 is next, and so on. Leave **Show on the website**
turned on. Click **Save**. New pick is live in about a minute."

---

## 5. "Hide a pick without deleting it" (~30 sec)
"If you want to take a pick off the website but keep it for later, click **This Season's Picks**,
click the pick, and find the **Show on the website?** switch — turn it **off**, then **Save**. It
disappears from your site but stays in your list, so you can turn it back on anytime."

---

## 6. (Optional) "Edit your story" (~45 sec)
"To change your story: on the left, click **About / Your Story**. The big box is **Your story** —
it types just like an email or a Word document; write as much as you like. You can also change the
**Big quote** at the top and your **name** at the bottom. Click **Save** when you're happy."

---

### What NOT to film (these are your "2 edits/month done-for-you")
Don't teach: adding pages, changing colors/fonts, layout, or anything structural. Those are the
*scary, call-generating* tasks — keep them as the done-for-you part of the care plan so Sandy
never attempts them. If she asks, the answer is "just send me a note and I'll do that one for you."
